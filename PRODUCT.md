# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two primary audiences: (1) Investigative journalists, NGOs, and data journalism organizations (like ICIJ peers, newsrooms, nonprofits) looking for a specialist who can handle complex document analysis, graph databases, and OSINT at the scale of Pulitzer-winning projects. (2) Startups and tech companies needing freelance data engineering or analytics work. Both audiences are technically literate and vet portfolios critically — they want evidence, not marketing copy.

## Product Purpose

A personal freelance portfolio for Miguel Fiandor, Data Analyst & Engineer. The site showcases 11+ years of specialized experience in ETL development, data journalism (ICIJ Panama Papers, Paradise Papers, FinCEN Files, Pandora Papers), OSINT, graph databases (Neo4j), and data visualization. Its job is to convert a visitor's interest into a contact or engagement — not to explain what data engineering is, but to demonstrate unusual depth in a specific niche.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, curious, accessible. Not self-promotional — the work is extraordinary and should let that carry. Tone is confident without being loud: a specialist who knows what they're doing and finds the work genuinely interesting.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS / startup landing page: cream or white background with teal gradients, big hero CTAs, identical icon+heading+text card grids, "We help companies achieve X" copywriting energy.
- Flashy developer portfolio: neon on black, particle or WebGL animations, full-page interactive JS experiments, oversized typing animations.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence over assertion**: Every claim has a link, a dataset, or a project name behind it. Don't describe capabilities — demonstrate them.
2. **Precision as personality**: The same rigor applied to data pipelines applies to layout and type. Consistency and care read as competence.
3. **Depth that doesn't gatekeep**: Expert content presented accessibly — not dumbed down, but not impenetrable. Journalists and engineers both need to feel at home.
4. **Bilingual without fragmentation**: English and Spanish feel equally first-class. Not a translation afterthought.
5. **Quiet confidence**: No hype language. The Panama Papers speak louder than any headline claim.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Bilingual content (English/Spanish) requires careful language-switching UX. No specific known user needs beyond standard responsive and keyboard accessibility.
